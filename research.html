Research

My research investigates activity‑dependent neuroplasticity in the ageing auditory system, with a focus on identifying the earliest points of failure that initiate downstream decline. I study both acute cochlear trauma in Noise‑Induced Hearing Loss (NIHL) and chronic degenerative processes in Age‑Related Hearing Loss (ARHL), using structural and electrophysiological approaches to uncover early cellular markers of vulnerability and mechanistic routes that may be therapeutically targeted.

Guided by a systems‑level perspective, my career goal is to understand how initial peripheral changes in ageing alter auditory function and listening behaviour, aiming to define therapeutic targets and intervention windows before widespread deterioration occurs.

Current Projects

Project 1: Early cellular markers in ARHL

Using mouse model with late-onset ARHL, I showed that structural and electrophysiological decline in inner hair cells (IHCs) can precede detectable loss of hearing thresholds. This work (submitted) expands prevailing views of the earliest sites of vulnerability in ageing and supports IHC deficits as a distinct pathological route.

Project 2: Noise-accelerated ARHL and shared therapeutic targets

In a study of noise-accelerated ageing, I found that NIHL and ARHL can follow distinct pathological trajectories despite superficially similar pathology and outcomes. In this project, I have identified an ion channel in the auditory receptor as a potential therapeutic target across both conditions and established evidence that cochlear outer hair cells (OHCs) are specific targets of noise-induced damage during ageing (manuscript in preparation).

Project 3: Methods and research integrity in auditory science

In parallel with biological discovery, I pursue technical inquiries that strengthen inference and reproducibility. This includes establishing the feasibility of Super-resolution Shadow Imaging (SUSHI) to assess morphological damage in NIHL, and work on whether residual ketamine from audiometric testing affects NIHL outcomes.
